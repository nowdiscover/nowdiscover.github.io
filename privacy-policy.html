Privacy Policy

Datenschutz, Achtung!

1. The purpose of this policy

The website and service of NowDiscover is directed towards businesses and professionally operating individuals. As a Berlin-based company we act in accordance with the German Federal Data Protection Act (Bundesdatenschutzgesetz, BDSG). The law considers any identified or identifiable individual as a “data subject“ whose “personal data” is deemed worth protecting against any form of impairment. The aim of this policy is to make it as transparent as possible how we at NowDiscover handle your and anyone else’s personal data.

2. What happens to information we receive?

2.1 Information you submit to us

You do not have to submit any personal data to us in order to use our site. Only when you sign up for the upcoming beta test of our service, do we ask you to submit your- Email Addressand consequentially your- Name
- Address
- Phone Number
- Additional Detailsfor on-boarding purposes.

By submitting your personal data you consent to the transfer, storage and processing of it by NowDiscover indefinitely. We apply technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data from unauthorized access. If you wish to amend or delete your personal data you can contact us at any time. Contact information can be found on our Imprint.

2.2 Information your internet device gives to us

You probably already know that while you are visiting websites on the internet your internet device has to communicate with website servers to make that happen. Our website is no different. As this communication contains valuable information to improve your personal user experience, we store the following information from it:- IP-Address
- Website you visited prior to our site
- Time of your visit
- Frequency of your visit
- Duration of your visit
- Browser Type
- Operating System
- Type of Device you use
- Geo-Location from where you visit us

3. How we use this information

3.1 When we want to communicate with you

are many reasons why we would want to communicate with you. The most important ones are:

1. To seek your participation in surveys, conduct them with you and analyze the responses later on.

2. To communicate about the beta test with you. Before we get you set up, during its run, to check in with you how things are going and afterwards to make sense of all the valuable feedback you provide to us.

3. To provide you with technical support whenever it is needed. We give our best. Have you tried to turn it off and on again?

4. To notify you about updates of our service or company. If you do not want to receive our news anymore, you can simply write us an email at unsubscribe@nowdiscover.com and we will stop with it immediately.

3.2 Improving your experience

We never stop improving our service. Therefore we analyze how you and other people use our website. We also look at your personal data to ask ourselves and test if it makes sense to personalize certain information, offers or parts of our experience to you.

3.3 When we aggregate data in our research

We may aggregate some parts of your personal data with other data to conduct statistical analyses and other research. During this process we ensure to transform your personal data into non-personal data in such a way that any conclusion, which we are able to make from the analysis, cannot be traced back to you or any other individual.

4. Sharing of information with others

4.1 Professional Service Providers

Some parts of our website and service involve the products and services of other companies. In order for these partner companies to perform it might be necessary to share certain information with them that may contain your personal data.

Whenever this is the case we only share as much of your data as it is necessary for our partners to provide their services. In such a case we always keep ownership of the data and ensure that it is deleted when we end our collaboration with them.

All of our partners operate under professional contract agreements that ensure organizational and technical security measures to protect them against unauthorized disclosure of their data.

At no point do we sell or exchange any personal data so that a third party company can make use of it on their behalf.

4.2 When the law asks us to do so

Certain laws, legal requirements and court orders can enforce us to share some or all of your personal data with the authorities or other legally entitled third parties.

4.3. In the context of a business transfer

In case we merge our business with another one, a company acquires substantially all of our business, or in the event of insolvency we may transfer your personal data to the party that is legally entitled to receive it.

4.4 International data transfers

For some of our activities it may be necessary to transfer your personal data to systems that are operating in other countries of the European Union (EU) or the United States of America (USA). We ensure that the applicable privacy laws of other EU-member-states or the USA are enacted. However we want to inform you that the privacy laws of other EU-member-states or the USA are not identical to the German Federal Data Protection Act (BDSG).

5. Cookies

Cookies are small text files placed on your internet device by the websites you visit. They can be read back by these websites to help them identify you when you return. A lot of the functionality of modern websites and web services would not work without them. Our Website uses cookies like many websites do.

If you use our website without blocking or disabling cookies, you indicate your consent to our use of them.

6. Web Tracking

6.1 Google Analytics

This website is using the service Google Analytics of Google Inc. to analyze user behavior. Google Analytics is using an extra cookie to generate information about this analysis that may be transferred to servers of Google within the USA.

In such a case, as the Google Analytics tracking of our website makes use of the so called IP masking function „gat._anonymizeIp();“ your personal IP address will be anonymized within one of the member states of the EU or the European Economic Area (EEA) before this transfer to Google servers in the USA actually occurs.

Google then will use this information on behalf of NowDiscover to analyze your use of this website, create research reports about it and to enable other Google services technically to function.

Your IP address never will be merged or consolidated with other data of Google.

In case you do not want Google Analytics to operate with any of your data you can de-activate the Google Analytics service in several ways:

(1) By using the „Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on“ which you can download and install following this link.

(2) By creating a cookie which indicates the Google Analytics service to not track your data by clicking at this link: De-activate Google Analytics

You can find more information to the terms of Google Analytics in regards to the German BDSG in German at http://www.google.com/analytics/terms/de.html and at https://www.google.de/intl/de/policies/.

6.2 Optimizely

This website is using the web analysis service of Optimizely Inc. (Optimizely) to improve the content of this website for different kind of users. Optimizely is using an extra cookie to generate information about this analysis that may be transferred to servers of Optimizely within the USA. In case you do not want Optimizely to operate with any of your data you can de-activate the collection, storage and usage of data for future activities by unsubscribing following this link.

You can find more information to the terms of Optimizely in regards to privacy matters at https://www.optimizely.com/privacy.
